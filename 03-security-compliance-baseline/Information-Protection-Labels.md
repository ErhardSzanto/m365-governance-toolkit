# Information Protection Labels (Taxonomy)

## Public
**Examples:** public website content, published brochures  
**Sharing:** allowed publicly  
**Controls:** no special restrictions

## Internal
**Examples:** internal procedures, meeting notes, internal announcements  
**Sharing:** internal users only by default; external sharing needs business justification  
**Controls:** encourage internal sharing, discourage external links

## Confidential
**Examples:** contracts, HR-like information, operational sensitive data  
**Sharing:** limited internal access; external sharing requires approval and time-bound guests  
**Controls:** label required; DLP warnings/blocks for external sharing

## Highly Confidential
**Examples:** highly sensitive personal/medical-like data, security-sensitive documents  
**Sharing:** restricted to need-to-know; external sharing generally prohibited unless exceptional approval  
**Controls:** strict access, no anonymous links, enforced protections where possible
